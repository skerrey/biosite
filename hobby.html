Quilting

Jan spends her time with two types of quilting styles, traditional and modern. Traditional uses standard patterns and designs. Modern is like textile art. The fabrics are also different. Traditional fabric is bought pre-colored and patterned at the store rather than dyed at home like with modern styles. Here are the steps she takes when quilting:

  1. Design the pattern
  2. Draw it
  3. Take pattern to make watercolor image (with watercolors) to get color she wants in pattern
  4. Make a multiple watercolor images to see possible variations
  5. Dye the fabric to match the colors in the watercolor
  6. Wait a couple days for fabric to dry
  7. Make pattern of images from the sketches (each color needs its own square of sturdy paper like a blueprint)
  8. Enlarge the watercolors by using a printer, sometimes to match the size of the fabric quilt
  9. Take each of the shapes that were cut out of different colors and sow them together to match watercolor blueprint

The quilt is done!

Travel

Jan likes to experience different cultures, and has done so her various travels. The top three places she has visited were Tuscany, Italy; Shaing Hai, China; Wyoming. During her travels she likes to talk to the people where she is visiting. She finds out what is important to the locals and what they like to do, and then participate in the local culture. Some of these things are:

  • live how the locals live
  • Eat local food
  • Local music
  • Cultural events
  • Plays
  • Being in the outdoors
  • Seeing nature

Shaing Hai Saw all the parks (there are a lot) Ate lots of Chinese food Learned to speak some Chinese

Jazz

Jan loves jazz. Although she doesn't play, she enjoys to listen. Story: “When I was in college I took a jazz class, jazz appreciation, I had a really good teacher and fell in love with jazz because of that class. I learned all the history of jazz, where it originated, the different types of jazz, and how they evolved. And we listened to jazz music every day in class, and I fell in love with it. And with that experience I have listened to jazz and gone to jazz concerts for the last 35 years. And got others interested in jazz.”

Some of her favorite artists are:

  • Dave Brubeck / Take 5
  • Miles davis / So What
  • Weather Report / Birdland
  • Chet Baker / My Funny Valentine
  • Herbie Hancock / Watermelon Man
